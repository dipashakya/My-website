About

With over two decades spanning architecture, urban planning, and academic research, I bring both the discipline of a practitioner and the rigor of a scholar to questions of heritage, place, and the built environment.

Rooted in Patan — one of the Kathmandu Valley's richest historic urban cores — my work is shaped by a deep engagement with Newar craft traditions, living heritage, and the urgent demands of post-earthquake reconstruction.

I have served as Deputy Head of the Architecture Department at Pulchowk Campus, co-ordinated the MSc in Urban Planning programme, and led multidisciplinary design projects across Nepal, Japan, China, Spain, and Italy.

My research asks how traditional craft knowledge — embodied, tacit, and community-held — can be systematically integrated into contemporary conservation practice.

Craft Conservation and Tourism Development (A case in Lalitpur Core, Patan)
Dipa Shakya & Sudarshan Raj Tiwari — Department of Architecture, T.U. IOE Pulchowk Campus
This research explores the need for craft conservation in the Lalitpur core — a district characterised by historic urban cultural heritage and distinguished craft heritage — examining how tangible and intangible heritage as human creativity, craft skill, and festivals can be strengthened as a 'Craft Town'. Through a survey of urban craft potential areas including Bhimsenbahal, Chunamhal Sundhara, Haugal Tichhu Gully, and Patan Durbar Square, the study analyses the local needs of people and concludes that conservation, public participation, and tourism together can ultimately sustain local development by making Lalitpur a model craft town.
